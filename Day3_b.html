Aspect Semantic Tags Asemantic Tags
Definition Provide meaning or context to content. Don't inherently convey meaning or context.
Purpose Used to structure content for search engines and assistive technologies. Primarily for formatting and styling purposes.
Examples <header>, <footer>, <article>, <nav>, <section>, <aside>, <main> <div>, <span>, <b>, <i>, <u>, <font>, <center>
Accessibility Enhance accessibility by providing semantic meaning to content. Don't inherently improve accessibility, may require additional ARIA attributes for accessibility.
SEO Benefits Improve search engine optimization (SEO) by providing clear structure and meaning. Less impact on SEO as search engines prioritize semantic content.
HTML5 Support Introduced and encouraged in HTML5, leading to improved web semantics. Supported across HTML versions but less emphasized in earlier versions.
